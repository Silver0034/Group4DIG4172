10 Facts about Puppy Mills by dosomething.org

What exactly is a puppy mill? Why is it bad? Here are 11 Facts as to why you should care about Ending puppy mills.

  1. A puppy mill is a commercial dog-breeding facility that focuses on profit. The health and welfare of the animals is not a priority.
  2. Female dogs are bred at every opportunity with minimum recovery time. When they are physically depleted to the point that they no longer can reproduce, breeding females are often killed.
  3. Every year in America, it is estimated that 2.11 million puppies are sold originated from puppy mills, while 3 million are killed in shelters because they are too full.
  4. In puppy mills, dogs can spend most of their lives in cramped cages, with no room to play or exercise.
  5. Often times, the water and food provided for the puppies is contaminated, crawling with bugs. Puppies can even be malnourished.
  6. In most states, puppy mills are legal.
  7. It is estimated that there are at least 10,000 puppy mills in the U.S. while fewer than 3,000 of these are regulated by the U.S. Department of Agriculture.
  8. Most puppy mills have no veterinary care, climate control, or protection for the animals from weather (hot, cold, rain, or snow).
  9. With limited or no regulations or enforcement, puppy mills have no cleanup control. This means that dogs can be living in urine and feces for indefinite periods of time.
  10. It's common to find dogs in puppy mills with collars that have been fastened so tightly that they have become embedded in a dog’s neck and must be carefully cut out.
